Snorkel Lahaina

Maui's west and northwest coasts offer some of the best snorkeling in the world. Be on the lookout for sea turtles, especially on the sand blending in under large rocks. They come up for air every few minutes and will often continue swimming near you if you are calm. Remember they are endagered, so be respectful and don't touch or harass them.

Road to Hana

The historic Road to Hana is a gorgeous winding road where you'll find lush greenery, multiple waterfalls, and plenty of worthwhile roadside stops like various amazing foods stands, the Black Sand Beach, and Waioka Pond. Plan in advance and make sure to come equipped with a reliable vehicle with a full gas tank; it's a full day trip and there are not many services along the way. Definitely an amazing trip with a convertable rental.

Breweries and Eats

Maui is home to some of the best restaurants and breweries. Maui Brewing is a popular hot spot with three locations on the island, and with good reason. However, Waikiki Brewing and Kilokilo Brewing are amazing local breweries that are worth a visit. Waikiki brewing even has beachfront seating and a great menu so you can relax and dine to the sound of the waves.

Black Sand Beach

Maui's famous Honokalani Beach boasts beautiful black sand that you'll rarely see elsewhere in the world. This is a must-see stop toward the end of the Road to Hana. However, you must make specifically timed reservations at least a few days in advane through Waianapanapa State Park, and the beach closes in the early afternoon so it is recommended to start your Hana trip early enough to make it.

Waioka Pond (Venus Pools)

Waioka Pond, known as the "Venus Pools" to tourists, are a series of three cascading pools connected by scenic waterfalls. This is Maui's most popular local spot for cliff jumping, with various heights to jump from depending on your courage. Waioka Pond is difficult to find, with rare roadside parking and requiring a short walk through dense flora to reach. This also means that there are usually only a few families there at a time, so it's relatively uncrowded. You might have to ask a local for precise location of the trailhead, but make sure to ask about Waioka Pond. It is highly offensive to refer to them as the Venus Pools in Hawaiian culture.
